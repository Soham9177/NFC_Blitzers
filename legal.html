Taxation Rules: Your Guide to Tax Laws and Compliance.

  • Income Tax: Income tax is a direct tax imposed on individuals, Hindu Undivided Families (HUFs), companies, and other entities. The rates vary depending on the income slab and the type of taxpayer. Income tax returns need to be filed annually.
  • Goods and Services Tax (GST): GST is an indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services. It replaced several indirect taxes, such as VAT, central excise duty, and service tax. GST rates are categorized into five slabs - 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%, with some items being exempted.
  • Corporate Tax: Companies in India are subject to corporate income tax. The rates may vary based on the size of the company and other factors.
  • Wealth Tax: As of my last update in 2021, wealth tax had been abolished in India. However, individuals are required to pay income tax on income generated from their wealth.
  • Property Tax: Property tax is imposed by local municipal authorities on property owners based on the value of their property. Rates and rules may vary from one municipality to another.
  • Capital Gains Tax: Capital gains tax is levied on the profit earned from the sale of capital assets like stocks, real estate, or other investments. There are short-term and long-term capital gains tax rates.
  • Securities Transaction Tax (STT): STT is a tax imposed on the purchase or sale of securities such as stocks, derivatives, and equity-oriented mutual funds in India.
